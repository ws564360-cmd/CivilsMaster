🏦 Banking System in India (భారత బ్యాంకింగ్ వ్యవస్థ)

The banking system plays an important role in economic development by accepting deposits, providing loans and supporting financial activities.

తెలుగులో: బ్యాంకింగ్ వ్యవస్థ ప్రజల పొదుపులను సేకరించి రుణాలు అందించడం ద్వారా ఆర్థిక అభివృద్ధికి సహాయపడుతుంది.

1. Reserve Bank of India (RBI)

  • RBI is the central bank of India.
  • Established on 1 April 1935.
  • Nationalised in 1949.
  • Headquarters: Mumbai.

Functions of RBI

  • Issue of currency notes.
  • Banker to Government.
  • Banker's bank.
  • Controls monetary policy.
  • Regulates banking system.
  • Manages foreign exchange reserves.

తెలుగులో: RBI భారతదేశ కేంద్ర బ్యాంకు. ఇది ద్రవ్య విధానాన్ని నియంత్రిస్తుంది.

2. Types of Banks in India

Commercial Banks

Provide banking services like deposits and loans to individuals and businesses.

Cooperative Banks

Serve agricultural and rural sectors.

Regional Rural Banks (RRBs)

Focus on rural development and farmers.

3. Monetary Policy Tools

  • Repo Rate: Rate at which RBI lends money to banks.
  • Reverse Repo Rate: Rate at which RBI borrows money from banks.
  • CRR: Cash Reserve Ratio maintained by banks with RBI.
  • SLR: Statutory Liquidity Ratio maintained by banks.

4. Banking Reforms

  • Nationalisation of Banks in 1969.
  • Financial Inclusion initiatives.
  • Digital banking development.
  • Promotion of banking access in rural areas.

5. Financial Inclusion

Financial inclusion means providing banking services to all sections of society.

  • Bank accounts
  • Credit facilities
  • Insurance services
  • Digital payments

UPSC Important Points 🎯

  • RBI is the central bank of India.
  • RBI controls monetary policy.
  • Repo rate is an important monetary tool.
  • Banking system supports economic growth.
  • Financial inclusion promotes inclusive development.
