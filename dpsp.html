📘 Directive Principles of State Policy

Directive Principles of State Policy (DPSP) are guidelines given to the government for establishing social and economic democracy in India.

తెలుగులో: రాజ్య విధాన ఆదేశిక సూత్రాలు (DPSP) భారతదేశంలో సామాజిక మరియు ఆర్థిక న్యాయం సాధించడానికి ప్రభుత్వానికి మార్గదర్శకాలు.

Constitutional Provision

  • DPSP are mentioned in Part IV of the Indian Constitution.
  • Articles 36 to 51 deal with DPSP.
  • Borrowed from the Irish Constitution.

తెలుగులో: DPSP రాజ్యాంగంలోని నాలుగవ భాగం (Part IV) లో ఉన్నాయి. ఇవి ఐర్లాండ్ రాజ్యాంగం నుండి తీసుకోబడ్డాయి.

Features of DPSP

  • They are non-justiciable.
  • They act as guidelines for government policies.
  • They aim to establish a welfare state.
  • They promote social and economic equality.

తెలుగులో: DPSP లను కోర్టులో అమలు చేయలేము, కానీ మంచి పాలన కోసం ప్రభుత్వానికి మార్గదర్శకాలుగా పనిచేస్తాయి.

Important Articles of DPSP

  • Article 38: Promote welfare of people
  • Article 39: Principles of policy
  • Article 39A: Equal justice and free legal aid
  • Article 40: Organisation of village panchayats
  • Article 44: Uniform Civil Code
  • Article 45: Early childhood care and education
  • Article 48: Agriculture and animal husbandry
  • Article 50: Separation of judiciary from executive
  • Article 51: International peace and security

Classification of DPSP

1. Socialist Principles

Aim to provide social and economic justice.

2. Gandhian Principles

Based on Gandhian ideas like village panchayats and rural development.

3. Liberal-Intellectual Principles

Focus on uniform civil code, environment protection and international peace.

Fundamental Rights vs DPSP 🎯

Fundamental Rights DPSP
Part III Part IV
Justiciable Non-justiciable
Political Democracy Social and Economic Democracy

UPSC Important Points

  • DPSP concept borrowed from Ireland.
  • Articles 36-51 are important for Prelims.
  • Article 32 and DPSP relationship is important for Mains.
  • DPSP aims to establish a welfare state.
