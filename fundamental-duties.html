🇮🇳 Fundamental Duties (మౌలిక విధులు)

Fundamental Duties are moral obligations mentioned in the Indian Constitution to remind citizens about their responsibilities towards the nation.

తెలుగులో: మౌలిక విధులు అనేవి పౌరులు దేశం పట్ల నిర్వహించాల్సిన బాధ్యతలను గుర్తు చేసే రాజ్యాంగ నిబంధనలు.

Constitutional Provision

  • Fundamental Duties are mentioned in Part IV-A of the Constitution.
  • Article 51A deals with Fundamental Duties.
  • Added by the 42nd Constitutional Amendment Act, 1976.
  • One more duty was added by the 86th Constitutional Amendment Act, 2002.

తెలుగులో: మౌలిక విధులను 1976లో 42వ రాజ్యాంగ సవరణ ద్వారా చేర్చారు. ప్రస్తుతం మొత్తం 11 విధులు ఉన్నాయి.

11 Fundamental Duties

  1. Respect the Constitution, National Flag and National Anthem.
  2. Follow the ideals of the freedom struggle.
  3. Protect the sovereignty, unity and integrity of India.
  4. Defend the country and render national service when required.
  5. Promote harmony and brotherhood among all people.
  6. Preserve the rich heritage of India's culture.
  7. Protect the natural environment including forests, lakes and wildlife.
  8. Develop scientific temper, humanism and spirit of inquiry.
  9. Safeguard public property and avoid violence.
  10. Strive for excellence in all fields.
  11. Provide opportunities for education to children between 6 and 14 years (by parents/guardians).

Important Points for UPSC 🎯

  • Fundamental Duties are not directly enforceable by courts.
  • They are inspired by the Constitution of USSR.
  • Originally there were 10 Fundamental Duties.
  • Presently there are 11 Fundamental Duties.
  • Article 51A contains all Fundamental Duties.

Fundamental Rights and Duties Relationship

Fundamental Rights provide freedoms to citizens, while Fundamental Duties remind citizens about their responsibilities.

తెలుగులో: మౌలిక హక్కులు పౌరులకు స్వేచ్ఛను ఇస్తాయి. మౌలిక విధులు పౌరుల బాధ్యతలను గుర్తు చేస్తాయి.
