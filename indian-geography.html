🇮🇳 Indian Geography (భారత భూగోళ శాస్త్రం)

Indian Geography deals with the physical features, climate, rivers, resources, agriculture and human activities of India.

తెలుగులో: భారత భూగోళ శాస్త్రంలో భారతదేశ భౌగోళిక స్వరూపం, నదులు, వాతావరణం, వ్యవసాయం మరియు వనరులను అధ్యయనం చేస్తారు.

1. Location and Size of India

  • India is located in South Asia.
  • Area: About 3.28 million square kilometres.
  • India is the 7th largest country in the world.
  • Indian Standard Time is based on 82.5° East longitude.

2. Physiographic Divisions of India

  • The Himalayan Mountains
  • Northern Plains
  • Peninsular Plateau
  • Indian Desert
  • Coastal Plains
  • Islands

తెలుగులో: భారతదేశ భౌగోళిక విభజనలు హిమాలయాలు, ఉత్తర మైదానాలు, ద్వీపకల్ప పీఠభూమి, ఎడారి, తీర మైదానాలు.

3. Himalayas (హిమాలయాలు)

  • Young fold mountains.
  • Highest mountain range in the world.
  • Important ranges: Himadri, Himachal, Shiwalik.

4. Northern Plains (ఉత్తర మైదానాలు)

Formed by the rivers Indus, Ganga and Brahmaputra.

  • Very fertile agricultural region.
  • Important for Indian agriculture.

5. Rivers of India (భారత నదులు)

Himalayan Rivers

  • Ganga
  • Yamuna
  • Brahmaputra

Peninsular Rivers

  • Godavari
  • Krishna
  • Kaveri
  • Narmada
  • Tapti

6. Climate of India

  • India has a Monsoon type climate.
  • South-West Monsoon brings most rainfall.
  • Monsoon is important for agriculture.

7. Soils of India

  • Alluvial Soil
  • Black Soil
  • Red Soil
  • Laterite Soil
  • Desert Soil

8. Agriculture in India

  • Rice
  • Wheat
  • Cotton
  • Sugarcane
  • Tea
  • Coffee

UPSC Important Points 🎯

  • India is the 7th largest country by area.
  • 82.5°E longitude is India's Standard Meridian.
  • Godavari is the longest river of Peninsular India.
  • Black soil is suitable for cotton cultivation.
  • Indian climate is influenced by monsoon.
