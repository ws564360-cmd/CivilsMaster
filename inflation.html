📈 Inflation & Monetary Policy (ద్రవ్యోల్బణం మరియు ద్రవ్య విధానం)

Inflation refers to a sustained increase in the general price level of goods and services in an economy.

తెలుగులో: వస్తువులు మరియు సేవల ధరలు నిరంతరం పెరగడాన్ని ద్రవ్యోల్బణం (Inflation) అంటారు.

1. Types of Inflation

Demand Pull Inflation

Occurs when demand for goods and services increases faster than supply.

తెలుగులో: డిమాండ్ ఎక్కువగా ఉండి సరఫరా తక్కువగా ఉన్నప్పుడు ఈ ద్రవ్యోల్బణం ఏర్పడుతుంది.

Cost Push Inflation

Occurs due to increase in production costs like raw materials and wages.

2. Causes of Inflation

  • Increase in demand
  • Increase in production costs
  • Excess money supply
  • Supply shortages
  • Increase in fuel prices

3. Effects of Inflation

  • Reduces purchasing power of money.
  • Affects savings.
  • Increases cost of living.
  • Creates economic uncertainty.

4. Measurement of Inflation

Consumer Price Index (CPI)

CPI measures changes in prices of goods and services consumed by people.

Wholesale Price Index (WPI)

WPI measures price changes at the wholesale level.

5. Monetary Policy (ద్రవ్య విధానం)

Monetary policy is used by RBI to control money supply, inflation and economic stability.

తెలుగులో: డబ్బు సరఫరా మరియు ద్రవ్యోల్బణాన్ని నియంత్రించడానికి RBI ద్రవ్య విధానాన్ని ఉపయోగిస్తుంది.

6. Tools of Monetary Policy

Repo Rate

Interest rate at which RBI lends money to commercial banks.

Reverse Repo Rate

Interest rate at which RBI borrows money from banks.

Cash Reserve Ratio (CRR)

Percentage of deposits banks must keep with RBI.

Statutory Liquidity Ratio (SLR)

Percentage of deposits banks maintain in liquid assets.

7. RBI and Inflation Control

  • Increasing repo rate reduces money supply.
  • Reducing repo rate encourages borrowing.
  • RBI uses monetary policy to maintain price stability.

UPSC Important Points 🎯

  • RBI controls monetary policy in India.
  • CPI is the main measure of retail inflation.
  • Inflation reduces purchasing power.
  • Repo rate affects borrowing costs.
  • Price stability is an important objective of RBI.
