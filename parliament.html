🏛️ Parliament of India (భారత పార్లమెంట్)

The Parliament of India is the supreme legislative body of the country. It represents the will of the people and makes laws for the nation.

తెలుగులో: భారత పార్లమెంట్ దేశంలోని అత్యున్నత శాసన వ్యవస్థ. ఇది ప్రజల తరఫున చట్టాలను రూపొందిస్తుంది.

Constitutional Provision

  • Article 79 - Constitution of Parliament.
  • Articles 79 to 122 deal with Parliament.
  • Parliament consists of President, Rajya Sabha and Lok Sabha.

Composition of Parliament

1. President of India

The President is an integral part of Parliament and gives assent to bills.

2. Rajya Sabha (Council of States)

  • Upper House of Parliament.
  • Permanent House.
  • Members have a term of six years.
  • One-third members retire every two years.

3. Lok Sabha (House of the People)

  • Lower House of Parliament.
  • Members are directly elected by people.
  • Normal term is five years.
  • Can be dissolved before completion of term.

Sessions of Parliament

  • Budget Session
  • Monsoon Session
  • Winter Session

The President summons each House of Parliament.

Important Officers

Lok Sabha Speaker

The Speaker presides over Lok Sabha meetings and maintains order.

Rajya Sabha Chairman

The Vice-President of India is the ex-officio Chairman of Rajya Sabha.

Law Making Process

  1. Introduction of Bill
  2. Discussion and Debate
  3. Passing by Both Houses
  4. President's Assent
  5. Becomes an Act

Money Bill

  • Article 110 deals with Money Bill.
  • Money Bill can be introduced only in Lok Sabha.
  • Rajya Sabha can only recommend changes.

తెలుగులో: ద్రవ్య బిల్లు (Money Bill) లోక్‌సభలో మాత్రమే ప్రవేశపెట్టాలి.

Parliamentary Committees

  • Public Accounts Committee (PAC)
  • Estimates Committee
  • Committee on Public Undertakings

Powers and Functions of Parliament

  • Legislative Functions - Making laws
  • Financial Functions - Control over budget
  • Executive Control - Question Hour and debates
  • Constitutional Amendment Power
  • Electoral Functions

UPSC Important Points 🎯

  • Article 79 defines Parliament.
  • Rajya Sabha is a permanent house.
  • Lok Sabha controls Council of Ministers through confidence.
  • Money Bill is mentioned in Article 110.
  • Vice-President is Chairman of Rajya Sabha.
