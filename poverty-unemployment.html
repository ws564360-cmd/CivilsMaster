👥 Poverty & Unemployment (పేదరికం మరియు నిరుద్యోగం)

Poverty and unemployment are important social and economic challenges affecting development.

తెలుగులో: పేదరికం మరియు నిరుద్యోగం భారతదేశ అభివృద్ధికి ముఖ్యమైన సవాళ్లు.

1. Poverty (పేదరికం)

Poverty refers to a situation where people cannot meet basic needs such as food, shelter and clothing.

తెలుగులో: ఆహారం, నివాసం, దుస్తులు వంటి ప్రాథమిక అవసరాలను తీర్చుకోలేని పరిస్థితిని పేదరికం అంటారు.

2. Types of Poverty

  • Absolute Poverty: Lack of basic necessities.
  • Relative Poverty: Poverty compared with other sections of society.
  • Multidimensional Poverty: Includes health, education and living standards.

3. Causes of Poverty

  • Unemployment
  • Low income
  • Population growth
  • Lack of education
  • Low productivity

4. Poverty Measurement

Poverty is measured using indicators related to income, consumption and living conditions.

  • Poverty Line
  • Multidimensional Poverty Index (MPI)

5. Government Schemes for Poverty Reduction

  • Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (MGNREGA)
  • Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana
  • National Rural Livelihood Mission
  • Food Security Programmes

6. Unemployment (నిరుద్యోగం)

Unemployment refers to a situation where people willing and able to work do not get employment opportunities.

7. Types of Unemployment

Seasonal Unemployment

Occurs when work is available only during certain seasons.

Disguised Unemployment

More people are engaged in work than actually required.

Structural Unemployment

Occurs due to changes in economic structure and skills mismatch.

8. Employment Generation

  • Skill development
  • Industrial growth
  • Entrepreneurship promotion
  • Infrastructure development

UPSC Important Points 🎯

  • Poverty affects quality of life.
  • Unemployment reduces economic growth.
  • MGNREGA provides rural employment.
  • Education and skill development help reduce unemployment.
  • Inclusive growth is important for development.
