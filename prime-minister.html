🇮🇳 Prime Minister of India (ప్రధానమంత్రి)

The Prime Minister is the real executive head of the Indian government and the leader of the Council of Ministers.

తెలుగులో: భారతదేశంలో వాస్తవ కార్యనిర్వాహక అధికారి ప్రధానమంత్రి. ప్రధానమంత్రి మంత్రివర్గానికి నాయకత్వం వహిస్తారు.

Constitutional Provisions

  • Article 74 - Council of Ministers to aid and advise the President.
  • Article 75 - Appointment and other provisions of Ministers.
  • Part V of the Constitution deals with Union Executive.

Appointment of Prime Minister

The President appoints the Prime Minister. Normally, the leader of the majority party in Lok Sabha is appointed as Prime Minister.

తెలుగులో: లోక్‌సభలో మెజారిటీ ఉన్న పార్టీ నాయకుడిని రాష్ట్రపతి సాధారణంగా ప్రధానమంత్రిగా నియమిస్తారు.

Qualifications

  • Must be a citizen of India.
  • Must be a member of Lok Sabha or Rajya Sabha.
  • If not a member of Parliament, must become a member within six months.
  • Must fulfill conditions for becoming a Member of Parliament.

Powers and Functions of Prime Minister

1. Leader of Council of Ministers

The Prime Minister selects ministers and allocates portfolios.

2. Link Between President and Council of Ministers

The Prime Minister communicates decisions of the Council of Ministers to the President.

3. Leader of Cabinet

The Prime Minister presides over Cabinet meetings and guides government policies.

4. Leader of Parliament

The Prime Minister plays an important role in legislative activities.

5. International Role

The Prime Minister represents India in international forums and meetings.

Council of Ministers

  • Cabinet Ministers
  • Ministers of State
  • Deputy Ministers

The Council of Ministers is collectively responsible to the Lok Sabha.

తెలుగులో: మంత్రివర్గం మొత్తం లోక్‌సభకు సమిష్టిగా బాధ్యత వహిస్తుంది.

Prime Minister and President Relationship

  • President is the constitutional head.
  • Prime Minister is the real executive head.
  • President acts according to the aid and advice of the Council of Ministers.

UPSC Important Points 🎯

  • Article 74 deals with Council of Ministers.
  • Article 75 deals with appointment and responsibility of ministers.
  • Prime Minister is the leader of Lok Sabha majority party.
  • Council of Ministers is collectively responsible to Lok Sabha.
  • Prime Minister is the head of the Cabinet.
