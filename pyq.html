📜 Polity PYQ Practice Questions

Q1. Which of the following is a Fundamental Right under the Indian Constitution?

A) Right to Property
B) Right to Equality
C) Right to Work
D) Right to Adequate Livelihood

✅ Answer: B) Right to Equality

Explanation:
Fundamental Rights are mentioned in Part III of the Constitution (Articles 12-35).

Q2. Article 32 of the Constitution is related to:

A) Constitutional Remedies
B) Education
C) Emergency
D) Citizenship

✅ Answer: A) Constitutional Remedies

Explanation:
Dr. B.R. Ambedkar called Article 32 the "Heart and Soul of the Constitution".

Q3. The Directive Principles of State Policy are included in:

A) Part II
B) Part III
C) Part IV
D) Part V

✅ Answer: C) Part IV

Explanation:
DPSP are guidelines for the government to establish social and economic democracy.

Q4. The President of India is elected by:

A) Direct election
B) Electoral College
C) Parliament only
D) Supreme Court

✅ Answer: B) Electoral College

Explanation:
The President is elected by elected members of Parliament and State Legislative Assemblies.

Q5. The power of Judicial Review is exercised by:

A) Legislature
B) Executive
C) Judiciary
D) Election Commission

✅ Answer: C) Judiciary

Explanation:
Judicial Review allows courts to examine the constitutional validity of laws.

🏛️ History PYQ Practice Questions

Q1. The Harappan Civilization is also known as:

A) Vedic Civilization
B) Indus Valley Civilization
C) Mauryan Civilization
D) Gupta Civilization

✅ Answer: B) Indus Valley Civilization

Explanation:
The Harappan Civilization developed around the Indus River valley and was one of the world's earliest urban civilizations.

Q2. The Great Bath was found at:

A) Harappa
B) Mohenjo-daro
C) Lothal
D) Kalibangan

✅ Answer: B) Mohenjo-daro

Explanation:
The Great Bath is one of the important architectural features of the Indus Valley Civilization.

Q3. Ashoka belonged to which dynasty?

A) Gupta Dynasty
B) Maurya Dynasty
C) Chola Dynasty
D) Kushan Dynasty

✅ Answer: B) Maurya Dynasty

Explanation:
Ashoka was a famous Mauryan emperor known for spreading Buddhism.

Q4. The Battle of Plassey was fought in:

A) 1757
B) 1764
C) 1857
D) 1947

✅ Answer: A) 1757

Explanation:
The Battle of Plassey established British political influence in Bengal.

Q5. The Indian National Congress was established in:

A) 1885
B) 1905
C) 1919
D) 1942

✅ Answer: A) 1885

Explanation:
The Indian National Congress was founded in 1885 with the efforts of A.O. Hume and Indian leaders.

🌍 Geography PYQ Practice Questions

Q1. The Tropic of Cancer passes through how many Indian states?

A) 5 States
B) 8 States
C) 10 States
D) 12 States

✅ Answer: B) 8 States

Explanation:
The Tropic of Cancer passes through eight states of India from Gujarat to Mizoram.

Q2. The longest river in India is:

A) Godavari
B) Ganga
C) Krishna
D) Narmada

✅ Answer: B) Ganga

Explanation:
The Ganga is one of the most important river systems of India.

Q3. Black soil is most suitable for:

A) Cotton cultivation
B) Tea cultivation
C) Rice cultivation only
D) Jute cultivation

✅ Answer: A) Cotton cultivation

Explanation:
Black soil has high moisture retention capacity and is ideal for cotton.

Q4. The Western Ghats are recognized as:

A) Biodiversity hotspot
B) Desert region
C) Plain region
D) Cold desert

✅ Answer: A) Biodiversity hotspot

Explanation:
Western Ghats have rich biodiversity and many endemic species.

Q5. Indian monsoon is mainly influenced by:

A) Seasonal reversal of winds
B) Earthquakes
C) Volcanoes
D) Soil formation

✅ Answer: A) Seasonal reversal of winds

Explanation:
Monsoon is caused by differential heating of land and sea and seasonal wind changes.

💰 Economy PYQ Practice Questions

Q1. Inflation refers to:

A) General increase in price level
B) Decrease in money supply
C) Increase in rainfall
D) Reduction in population

✅ Answer: A) General increase in price level

Explanation:
Inflation reduces the purchasing power of money due to continuous increase in prices.

Q2. Reserve Bank of India was established in:

A) 1935
B) 1947
C) 1950
D) 1969

✅ Answer: A) 1935

Explanation:
RBI is India's central bank responsible for monetary policy.

Q3. GST is a:

A) Direct tax
B) Indirect tax
C) Corporate loan
D) Monetary policy

✅ Answer: B) Indirect tax

Explanation:
Goods and Services Tax is a unified indirect tax system introduced in India in 2017.

Q4. Fiscal deficit means:

A) Government expenditure exceeds revenue
B) Increase in exports
C) Decrease in production
D) Increase in savings

✅ Answer: A) Government expenditure exceeds revenue

Explanation:
Fiscal deficit shows the borrowing requirement of the government.

Q5. NITI Aayog replaced:

A) Planning Commission
B) Finance Commission
C) RBI
D) Election Commission

✅ Answer: A) Planning Commission

Explanation:
NITI Aayog was established in 2015 as a policy think tank.

📜 Part 1 : Constitutional Development PYQ

(భారత రాజ్యాంగ అభివృద్ధి PYQ విశ్లేషణ)

🎯 Introduction

English:
Constitutional Development is a core area of Indian Polity. UPSC frequently asks questions from British Acts, constitutional reforms and historical evolution of Indian administration.

తెలుగు:
భారత రాజ్యాంగ అభివృద్ధి భారత రాజ్యాంగంలో ముఖ్యమైన అంశం. బ్రిటిష్ చట్టాలు, రాజ్యాంగ సంస్కరణలు మరియు భారత పరిపాలనా పరిణామాల నుండి UPSC తరచుగా ప్రశ్నలు అడుగుతుంది.

📜 Constitutional Development PYQ Questions

Q1. Regulating Act was passed in:

A) 1773
B) 1784
C) 1813
D) 1858

✅ Answer: A) 1773

Explanation:
Regulating Act 1773 was the first step by the British Parliament to control the East India Company administration.

Q2. Pitt's India Act was introduced in:

A) 1773
B) 1784
C) 1833
D) 1909

✅ Answer: B) 1784

Explanation:
Pitt's India Act created a system of dual control between the British Government and East India Company.

Q3. Government of India Act 1858 transferred power from:

A) British Crown to Company
B) Company to British Crown
C) Parliament to Judiciary
D) Army to Civil Services

✅ Answer: B) Company to British Crown

Explanation:
After the Revolt of 1857, administration of India came directly under the British Crown.

Q4. Indian Councils Act 1909 is also known as:

A) Morley-Minto Reforms
B) Montagu-Chelmsford Reforms
C) Simon Reforms
D) Cripps Reforms

✅ Answer: A) Morley-Minto Reforms

Explanation:
The Act introduced separate electorates for Muslims.

Q5. Government of India Act 1935 introduced:

A) Provincial Autonomy
B) Fundamental Rights
C) Directive Principles
D) Republic System

✅ Answer: A) Provincial Autonomy

Explanation:
The Act introduced provincial autonomy and proposed an All India Federation.

📖 Part 2 : Making of Indian Constitution PYQ

(భారత రాజ్యాంగ నిర్మాణం PYQ విశ్లేషణ)

🎯 Introduction

English:
Questions from the making of the Constitution focus on the Constituent Assembly, committees, important personalities and constitutional sources.

తెలుగు:
రాజ్యాంగ నిర్మాణం నుండి వచ్చే ప్రశ్నలు రాజ్యాంగ పరిషత్, కమిటీలు, ప్రముఖ వ్యక్తులు మరియు రాజ్యాంగ మూలాలపై ఆధారపడి ఉంటాయి.

📖 Making of Constitution PYQ Questions

Q1. Constituent Assembly was formed under:

A) Cabinet Mission Plan 1946
B) Cripps Mission 1942
C) Simon Commission 1927
D) Indian Councils Act 1909

✅ Answer: A) Cabinet Mission Plan 1946

Explanation:
The Constituent Assembly was created according to the Cabinet Mission Plan of 1946.

Q2. The first meeting of Constituent Assembly was held on:

A) 9 December 1946
B) 15 August 1947
C) 26 November 1949
D) 26 January 1950

✅ Answer: A) 9 December 1946

Explanation:
The first meeting was held in New Delhi under the temporary chairmanship of Dr. Sachchidananda Sinha.

Q3. President of Constituent Assembly was:

A) Dr. Rajendra Prasad
B) Jawaharlal Nehru
C) B.R. Ambedkar
D) Sardar Patel

✅ Answer: A) Dr. Rajendra Prasad

Explanation:
Dr. Rajendra Prasad was elected as the permanent President of the Constituent Assembly.

Q4. Drafting Committee was headed by:

A) B.R. Ambedkar
B) Nehru
C) Patel
D) Rajendra Prasad

✅ Answer: A) B.R. Ambedkar

Explanation:
Dr. B.R. Ambedkar served as the Chairman of the Drafting Committee.

Q5. Indian Constitution was adopted on:

A) 26 November 1949
B) 15 August 1947
C) 26 January 1950
D) 9 December 1946

✅ Answer: A) 26 November 1949

Explanation:
The Constitution was adopted on 26 November 1949 and came into force on 26 January 1950.

🎯 Introduction

English:
Preamble and Fundamental Rights are highly important areas in UPSC Polity. Questions are frequently asked from constitutional values, Articles and Supreme Court judgments.

తెలుగు:
పీఠిక మరియు ప్రాథమిక హక్కులు UPSC రాజ్యాంగంలో అత్యంత ముఖ్యమైన అంశాలు. రాజ్యాంగ విలువలు, ఆర్టికల్స్ మరియు సుప్రీంకోర్టు తీర్పుల నుండి తరచుగా ప్రశ్నలు వస్తాయి.

⚖️ Preamble & Fundamental Rights PYQ Questions

Q1. The Preamble declares India as:

A) Sovereign Socialist Secular Democratic Republic
B) Federal Monarchy
C) Presidential State
D) Unitary Government

✅ Answer: A) Sovereign Socialist Secular Democratic Republic

Explanation:
The Preamble describes the nature of the Indian State and its objectives.

Q2. Fundamental Rights are mentioned in:

A) Part II
B) Part III
C) Part IV
D) Part V

✅ Answer: B) Part III

Explanation:
Articles 12 to 35 of the Constitution deal with Fundamental Rights.

Q3. Article 14 provides:

A) Equality before law
B) Freedom of religion
C) Right to education
D) Constitutional remedies

✅ Answer: A) Equality before law

Explanation:
Article 14 guarantees equality before law and equal protection of laws.

Q4. Right to Life and Personal Liberty is under:

A) Article 19
B) Article 21
C) Article 25
D) Article 32

✅ Answer: B) Article 21

Explanation:
Article 21 protects life and personal liberty of individuals.

Q5. Constitutional Remedies are provided under:

A) Article 32
B) Article 40
C) Article 50
D) Article 51

✅ Answer: A) Article 32

Explanation:
Dr. B.R. Ambedkar called Article 32 the "Heart and Soul of the Constitution".

📜 DPSP & Fundamental Duties PYQ Questions

Q1. Directive Principles of State Policy are mentioned in:

A) Part III
B) Part IV
C) Part V
D) Part VI

✅ Answer: B) Part IV

Explanation:
DPSP are contained in Articles 36 to 51 of the Indian Constitution.

Q2. DPSP are inspired from the Constitution of:

A) Ireland
B) USA
C) Canada
D) France

✅ Answer: A) Ireland

Explanation:
India borrowed the idea of Directive Principles from the Irish Constitution.

Q3. Fundamental Duties were added by:

A) 42nd Constitutional Amendment Act, 1976
B) 44th Amendment Act
C) 73rd Amendment Act
D) 86th Amendment Act

✅ Answer: A) 42nd Constitutional Amendment Act, 1976

Explanation:
Part IVA containing Article 51A was added by the 42nd Amendment.

Q4. Article 39A relates to:

A) Equal justice and free legal aid
B) Uniform Civil Code
C) Protection of environment
D) Village Panchayats

✅ Answer: A) Equal justice and free legal aid

Explanation:
Article 39A aims to provide equal justice and free legal aid.

Q5. Fundamental Duties are mentioned in:

A) Article 51A
B) Article 32
C) Article 21
D) Article 368

✅ Answer: A) Article 51A

Explanation:
Article 51A contains the Fundamental Duties of citizens.

🎯 Introduction

English:
The Union and State Executive is an important area of Indian Polity. UPSC asks questions on powers, elections, appointments and constitutional provisions.

🏛️ Executive PYQ Questions

Q1. The President of India is elected by:

A) Direct election by citizens
B) Electoral College
C) Parliament only
D) Supreme Court

✅ Answer: B) Electoral College

Explanation:
The President is elected by elected members of Parliament and State Legislative Assemblies.

Q2. Minimum age required to become President of India is:

A) 25 years
B) 30 years
C) 35 years
D) 40 years

✅ Answer: C) 35 years

Explanation:
Article 58 specifies qualifications for the office of President.

Q3. Governor of a State is appointed by:

A) Prime Minister
B) President of India
C) Chief Minister
D) Chief Justice of India

✅ Answer: B) President of India

Explanation:
The Governor is appointed by the President under Article 155.

Q4. President's ordinance making power is mentioned in:

A) Article 123
B) Article 143
C) Article 356
D) Article 368

✅ Answer: A) Article 123

Explanation:
Article 123 empowers the President to issue ordinances when Parliament is not in session.

Q5. The Governor's ordinance making power is under:

A) Article 213
B) Article 214
C) Article 216
D) Article 220

✅ Answer: A) Article 213

Explanation:
Article 213 provides ordinance making power to the Governor.

🏛️ Parliament PYQ Questions

Q1. The Prime Minister of India is appointed by:

A) President of India
B) Chief Justice of India
C) Lok Sabha Speaker
D) Governor

✅ Answer: A) President of India

Explanation:
The President appoints the Prime Minister and other ministers are appointed on PM's advice.

Q2. Council of Ministers is collectively responsible to:

A) Rajya Sabha
B) Lok Sabha
C) President
D) Supreme Court

✅ Answer: B) Lok Sabha

Explanation:
Article 75 states collective responsibility of the Council of Ministers to Lok Sabha.

Q3. Maximum strength of Lok Sabha is:

A) 545
B) 552
C) 500
D) 600

✅ Answer: B) 552

Explanation:
The Constitution originally provided a maximum strength of 552 members.

Q4. Money Bill can be introduced only in:

A) Lok Sabha
B) Rajya Sabha
C) Either House
D) Joint Sitting

✅ Answer: A) Lok Sabha

Explanation:
Article 110 defines Money Bill and it can be introduced only in Lok Sabha.

Q5. Joint sitting of Parliament is provided under:

A) Article 108
B) Article 110
C) Article 112
D) Article 123

✅ Answer: A) Article 108

Explanation:
Article 108 provides for joint sitting of both Houses of Parliament.
