⚖️ Supreme Court of India (సుప్రీం కోర్టు)

The Supreme Court of India is the highest judicial authority and the guardian of the Constitution.

తెలుగులో: భారత సుప్రీం కోర్టు దేశంలో అత్యున్నత న్యాయస్థానం. ఇది రాజ్యాంగానికి సంరక్షకుడిగా పనిచేస్తుంది.

Constitutional Provisions

  • Articles 124 to 147 deal with the Supreme Court.
  • Part V of the Constitution deals with Union Judiciary.
  • Supreme Court started functioning on 28 January 1950.

Composition of Supreme Court

  • Chief Justice of India (CJI)
  • Other Judges appointed by the President of India

The number of judges is determined by Parliament.

Appointment of Judges

Supreme Court judges are appointed by the President of India after consultation with judges of the Supreme Court and High Courts.

తెలుగులో: సుప్రీం కోర్టు న్యాయమూర్తులను భారత రాష్ట్రపతి నియమిస్తారు.

Qualifications of Supreme Court Judge

  • Must be a citizen of India.
  • Must have been a High Court judge for five years, or
  • Must have been an advocate of High Court for ten years, or
  • Must be a distinguished jurist.

Jurisdiction of Supreme Court

1. Original Jurisdiction (Article 131)

The Supreme Court directly hears disputes between the Centre and States.

2. Writ Jurisdiction (Article 32)

The Supreme Court can issue writs for enforcement of Fundamental Rights.

3. Appellate Jurisdiction

It hears appeals against judgments of High Courts.

4. Advisory Jurisdiction (Article 143)

The President can seek advice from the Supreme Court on important legal questions.

Important Powers

  • Judicial Review
  • Protection of Fundamental Rights
  • Guardian of Constitution
  • Final interpreter of Constitution

తెలుగులో: సుప్రీం కోర్టు రాజ్యాంగాన్ని రక్షిస్తుంది మరియు చట్టాల రాజ్యాంగబద్ధతను పరిశీలిస్తుంది.

Important Articles

  • Article 124 - Establishment of Supreme Court
  • Article 129 - Court of Record
  • Article 131 - Original Jurisdiction
  • Article 136 - Special Leave Petition
  • Article 141 - Law declared by Supreme Court binding on all courts
  • Article 143 - Advisory Jurisdiction

Judicial Review

Judicial Review is the power of the Supreme Court to examine the constitutional validity of laws and government actions.

తెలుగులో: చట్టాలు రాజ్యాంగానికి అనుగుణంగా ఉన్నాయా లేదా అని పరిశీలించే అధికారం న్యాయ సమీక్ష (Judicial Review).

UPSC Important Points 🎯

  • Supreme Court is the highest court of India.
  • Article 32 is called the Heart and Soul of Constitution.
  • Article 143 deals with Advisory Jurisdiction.
  • Supreme Court is the guardian of Constitution.
  • Judicial Review is an important feature of Indian Judiciary.
